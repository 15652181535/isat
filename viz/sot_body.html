Instructions

The satellites are displayed based on calculated position; you can watch them move but it helps to zoom in a bit. You may have to zoon out quite a bit to see satellites with very large orbits. You can rotate the Earth and zoom in or out. If you've allowed geolocation in your browser, a marker will show your current position.

Hover over a satelite icon to show its name. Click it to bring up pages/tabs that provide details from Science.nasa.gov and NSSDC. Select a specific satellite from a group to highlight it and move the camera to its position.
